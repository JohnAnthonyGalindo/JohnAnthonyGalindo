A little about me

I've spent most of my career in sales roles at tech companies, and somewhere along the way I became really interested in the people side of it. Not just hitting numbers, but understanding why some teams click and others don't, and what it takes to build something that actually lasts. That curiosity led me into managing and building an SDR team in legal tech, working as a Enterprise SDR in the database world, and to my current role as an Account Executive where I help companies retain, activate, and monetize their user base.

I also dabble in code (nothing crazy, but more than the average sales guy). I find that understanding software is important when you work in tech, and it's made me a better partner to the engineers and technical buyers I work with.

What I'm exploring

It's hard to ignore the AI boom in 2026. Claude has changed the way engineers ship code, and sales teams are delegating what feels like 90% of the outbound sales process to agents.

20% of AI applications make it out of pre-production (that's a generous estimate), yet every company is rethinking how AI will shape their workforce. To cut through the noise and track what's actually working, I've built a network of AI engineers and engineering leaders to stay up to date with current trends and use cases.

Beyond AI, I've made the strategic decision to expand my horizons by taking Spanish classes. Latin America is an often overlooked market, yet Mexico and Brazil alone pulled in an estimated $4.6B in VC funding in 2025. Major players like Plata, the Mexico-based FinTech company, raised a $410M Series C and CIMED Healthcare in Brazil is planning to invest $90 million to expand its industrial complex in Pouso Alegre, Minas Gerais.

I believe countries like Mexico, Brazil, Argentina, and Colombia are undergoing a renaissance unlike anything we've seen in the US. There's still a real cultural gap for companies selling into Latin American markets plus a technical communication layer that very few people can bridge. I'm not sure what the future holds, but I'm genuinely excited to see what comes out of this region.

Lastly, I'm exploring a few ideas I'd like to get hands-on and build. Once I have something worth sharing, I will. More to come.
